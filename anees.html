PKRSP


Pak-Kashmir Rural Support Program (PKRSP) was established in July 2010 in Azad Jammu & Kashmir to address the pressing needs of its 4.8 million residents, primarily those living in rural areas facing challenges of illiteracy, poverty, and underdevelopment.

Originating from a vision in 2003 by Mr. Tanveer Ahmad and his colleagues at the Community Organization of Barmala Hillan under the National Rural Support Program (NRSP), PKRSP began as a grassroots initiative aimed at empowering marginalized communities. Despite limited resources, the determination of its founders and support from local philanthropists and NGOs allowed PKRSP to grow into a recognized non-profit organization, officially registered with the Kashmir Council

achievements

Disability Projects

We successfully distributed 204 wheelchairs to individuals facing mobility challenges. Each wheelchair is more than just a mode of transportation; it symbolizes a vital step toward independence, & dignity.

Plantation

We distributed 16,000 ornamental plants, 172 fruit plants, improved seeds, and livestock to promote biodiversity and protect nature. Our volunteers provided essential vaccinations, fostering healthier communities and sustainability.

Social Safety Services

We supplied earthquake-affected people in 2005 with 250 tents, 2,700 steel sheets, and 350 blankets. We established three tent villages and distributed 1,000 clothing pairs in collaboration with Khidmat-e-Khalaq Foundation.
